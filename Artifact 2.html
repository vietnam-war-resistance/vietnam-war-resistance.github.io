Exhibit #2: Tinker v. Des Moines School Dist.

Link to full pdf

Tinker v. Des Moines School District was a major case that was based on First Amendment rights. John Tinker and his sister Mary wore peace armbands to school to protest the Vietnam War. The school district threatened to expel them if they didn’t stop wearing it immediately. Although they complied, they took it to court, arguing that the First Amendment gave them the right to protest at school. In 1968, they took it all the way up to the Supreme Court who, in a 7-2 decision, ruled in favor of the students. The judges said, “[i]t can hardly be argued that either students or teachers shed their constitutional rights to freedom of speech or expression at the schoolhouse gate.”
