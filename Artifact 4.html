Exhibit #4: Vietnam Veterans Against the War Patch

This is the badge, which originated in 1967, for the VVAW (Vietnam Veterans Against the War). It is extremely similar to the MACV (Military Assistance Command, Vietnam) insignia, except it has an upside down rifle with a helmet (a sign for soldiers killed in action) instead of a sword. In the MACV version, the yellow and red represent the colors of Vietnam, the wall refers to the Great Wall of China, and the sword alludes to US aid in Vietnam. The organization is dedicated to stopping the lies spread by the US and the MCAV about American intervention in Indochina.
