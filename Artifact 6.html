Exhibit #6: Coup in South Vietnam

This is a picture – taken by an unknown photographer – of the coup that took place in 1963 in South Vietnam. Ngô Đình Diệm was the president at the time and he was extremely unpopular with the citizens because he made decisions that restricted their lives. The only reason he was in power was because he was friendly with the US, so they helped him get the position. However, once they found out the citizens didn’t like him, the CIA incited a rebellion that led to his death. This is just one example of the common people coming together and taking action against US involvement in Vietnam.
