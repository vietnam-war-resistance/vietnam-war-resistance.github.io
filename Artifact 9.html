Exhibit #9: Vietnamese Buddhist Monk Self-Immolation

This is a picture of Vietnamese Buddhist monk Thích Quảng Đức burning himself alive as a form of protest against the Vietnam War. He lit himself aflame at a busy Saigon intersection to get maximum impact. Malcom Browne, an Associated Press photographer present at the time took this photo and won the 1963 World Press Photo of the Year ad inspired many monks and citizens alike to continue fighting for peace.
