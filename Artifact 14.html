Exhibit #14: Political Cartoon

Many newspapers published editorial cartoons to satirize US involvement in the Vietnam War. In the picture shown above, “a Viet Cong labeled ‘Guerrilla Warfare’ [is] standing at the edge of tall grass, pointing a handgun labeled ‘Conventional Arms’ at a startled President Lyndon Johnson who is carrying atomic bombs under his arms and a sign that states ‘Mightiest Nation in History.’” Edmund Valtman, the cartoonist who drew this in 1964, made fun of the US, which had developed advanced nuclear weapons, being undone by a gang of guerrillas with simple firearms. Other political cartoons also made fun of LBJ’s decisions to bomb near China, his justifications for attacking Vietnam, and escalating the conflict even further.
