Exhibit #12: The Armies of the Night

This book talks about the march on the Pentagon in October 1967. It recounts Norman Mailer’s, an author who was against the war, experience on the march and questions what America was fighting for in Vietnam. Like songs, books are a great way to spread the nonviolent peace movement because they are available to anyone and everyone. Plus, they can cause a sensation like Thomas Paine’s “Common Sense” and Harriet Beecher Stowe’s “Uncle Tom’s Cabin.” Norman Mailer, in particular, wrote many books about Vietnam War protests like “Why are We in Vietnam?” and “Miami and the Siege of Chicago.”
