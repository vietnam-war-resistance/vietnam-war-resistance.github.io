Exhibit #7: Martin Luther King Jr. giving the speech “Beyond Vietnam”

This picture shows Martin Luther King Jr. delivering his groundbreaking speech “Beyond Vietnam” at New York’s Riverside Church on April 4, 1967. In it, he talks about how it was essential that the US withdraw troops from Vietnam and stop the war. He said that there were more important matters than creating conflict in other countries, primarily resolving conflict at home – like segregation. Although he received lots of criticism for this fiery speech, “King continued to attack the Vietnam War on both moral and economic grounds.”
