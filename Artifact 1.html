Exhibit #1: Tonkin Gulf Resolution

Passed in 1964 by President Lyndon B. Johnson, the Tonkin Gulf Resolution was just the beginning of the many unjust actions that took place during the Vietnam War. It states that “the United States is assisting the peoples of southeast Asia to protest their freedom and has no territorial, military or political ambitions in the area…” However, the main reason that the US went to war was to prevent the domino effect – the idea that if one country fell to communism others would as well. This is the definition of a “political ambition” – the US is interfering in the politics and government of Vietnam. This single document caused thousands of deaths and hundreds of global protests.
