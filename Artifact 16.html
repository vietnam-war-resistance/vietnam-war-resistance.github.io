Exhibit #16: Anti-War GI Newspaper

This artifact is a magazine, published on April 28, 1971, that helped spread the word about the anti-war movements. Over 300 underground newspapers, like Fed Up, were published during the Vietnam War. These magazines were produced in GI coffeehouses, places where soldiers could relax and talk freely about the unfairness of it all. These newspapers spread “antiwar and radical ideas” secretly and convinced many people to fight back. However, many of these papers were short-lived because of the turnover throughout military bases. Still, they were a vital part of spreading the message of peace.
