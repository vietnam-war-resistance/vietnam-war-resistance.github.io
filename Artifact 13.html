Exhibit #13: Vietnam War Protest on the Capital

On May 1, 1971, thousands of people protested the Vietnam War in Washington D.C. Large protests had been done before, but it was mostly just “doing a little marching around, listening to speeches and music, and then getting back on the busses to go home. For some in the antiwar movement, this familiar routine — what one critic called ‘dull ceremonies of dissent’ — began to feel futile and too easily ignored by the Nixon administration.” So, Rennie Davis planned to organize a rally in which people would camp out and disrupt federal workers from getting to their jobs. And that’s just what he did. Although thousands of people got arrested for civil disobedience, it was hugely successful.
